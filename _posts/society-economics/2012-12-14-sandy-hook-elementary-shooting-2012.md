**Key figures**: Adam Lanza (perpetrator, age 20); 26 victims killed including 20 children ages 6–7 and 6 adult staff members

## Summary

On December 14, 2012, at approximately 9:35 AM, Adam Lanza fatally shot his mother, Nancy Lanza (52), at their home in Newtown, Connecticut, before driving to Sandy Hook Elementary School. Lanza then forced his way through a locked glass entrance door at the school and entered the building armed with a Bushmaster XM-15 semi-automatic rifle loaded with 10 thirty-round magazines. In the hallway, he fatally shot Principal Dawn Hochsprung and school psychologist Mary Sherlach. He then entered two first-grade classrooms (Rooms 8 and 10) and systematically fired 154 rounds over less than five minutes, killing 20 children—all first-graders, ages 6–7 (12 girls and 8 boys)—and four additional teachers: Lauren Rousseau, Rachel D'Avino, Victoria Leigh Soto, and Anne Marie Murphy. One six-year-old girl survived by hiding beneath a desk and feigning death. Lanza shot himself in the head at 9:40:03 AM. Police arrived 9–10 minutes after the shooting began. The total death toll was 27 (including the shooter): 1 mother, 20 children, and 6 school staff members. This was the second-deadliest school shooting in U.S. history, after Virginia Tech in 2007.

## Significance

Sandy Hook became the deadliest incident in Connecticut history and one of the most traumatic mass-shooting events in American collective memory. The tragedy, targeting a kindergarten classroom, reignited fierce national debate over gun control, universal background checks, and restrictions on semi-automatic rifles and high-capacity ammunition magazines. Advocates called for an assault-weapons ban and expanded mental-health screening. Opponents emphasized second-amendment rights and the inadequacy of school security measures. Though federal gun-control legislation failed to pass Congress, the tragedy led to state-level regulations and became a reference point for American gun-policy discourse that persisted for years. The original Sandy Hook Elementary School building was demolished and rebuilt in a new location. In response to the tragedy, several advocacy organizations were founded, most notably Sandy Hook Promise, which works to prevent school shootings through evidence-based programs and policy advocacy. The shooting also sparked international discussions about gun access and school safety and remains a watershed moment in discussions of American mass violence.
