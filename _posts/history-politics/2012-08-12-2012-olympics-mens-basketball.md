## Summary

The men's basketball tournament at the 2012 London Summer Olympics ran from July 29 to August 12 at the North Greenwich Arena (O2 Arena). Twelve national teams competed, divided into two groups of six, with the top four from each group advancing to knockout rounds. The United States, coached by Duke University's Mike Krzyzewski in his second Olympics as the national team's head coach, fielded a roster widely considered among the greatest ever assembled, winning all eight of its games en route to the gold medal.

The gold-medal game on August 12 saw the USA defeat Spain 107–100 — the closest contest of the American campaign. Kevin Durant led all scorers with 30 points, and LeBron James added 19. Spain, coached by Sergio Scariolo, was led by Pau Gasol's 24 points, with brother Marc Gasol and Serge Ibaka (born in Brazzaville, Republic of the Congo, and competing for Spain) also featuring in a formidable frontcourt. Russia defeated Argentina 81–77 in the bronze-medal game, also played on August 12.

## Team USA Roster and Key Stats

Team USA went 8–0 during the tournament, winning by an average margin of roughly 32 points. Carmelo Anthony set a U.S. Olympic single-game scoring record with 37 points against Nigeria on August 2 — a 156–73 rout that itself set U.S. Olympic records for total points and margin of victory. Kevin Durant averaged a team-high 19.5 points per game across the tournament, emerging as the team's leading scorer overall. LeBron James, fresh off his first NBA championship with the Miami Heat weeks earlier, averaged 13.3 points, 5.6 rebounds, and 4.6 assists.

The roster's depth was extraordinary: all twelve players were current or future NBA All-Stars. Coach Krzyzewski described the 2012 unit as "the best team I've ever coached," surpassing even the celebrated 2008 "Redeem Team" that ended the post-2004 gold-medal drought.

## Tournament Path

**Group Stage (July 29 – August 6):**
- July 29: USA 98, France 71
- July 31: USA 110, Tunisia 63
- August 2: USA 156, Nigeria 73 — a U.S. Olympic record for total points and margin
- August 4: USA 99, Lithuania 94 — the Americans' closest game of the tournament
- August 6: USA 126, Argentina 97

**Quarterfinals (August 8):**
- USA 119, Australia 86

**Semifinals (August 10):**
- USA 109, Argentina 83
- Spain 67, Russia 59

**Final (August 12):**
- USA 107, Spain 100

## Rival Teams

**Spain** was the most formidable challenger, reaching the final on the strength of an NBA-caliber frontcourt pairing in Pau and Marc Gasol and a versatile defense anchored by Ibaka. Their 107–100 loss was the closest any team came to defeating the Americans in the entire tournament.

**France**, led by Tony Parker (San Antonio Spurs) and Boris Diaw, fell to the USA 98–71 in the opening group game but showed the growing depth of European basketball before being eliminated by Spain in the quarterfinals.

**Argentina**, a Summer Olympics medal contender, still featured veterans of their legendary 2004 gold-medal team, including Luis Scola and Manu Ginóbili, and reached the semifinals before losing to the United States and then falling to Russia in the bronze-medal game.

## Context and Legacy

The 2012 tournament unfolded amid London's broader [Summer Olympic Games]({{ '/news/history-politics/london-2012-olympics/' | relative_url }}), which opened on July 27 with [Danny Boyle's celebrated opening ceremony]({{ '/news/history-politics/london-2012-opening-ceremony/' | relative_url }}). Basketball's high-profile matches drew large crowds to the 20,000-seat O2 Arena and attracted global television audiences, reinforcing the NBA's international marketing ambitions.

For Kobe Bryant, the tournament yielded his second Olympic gold medal (after 2008) and ultimately his final Olympic appearance; he retired from the NBA in April 2016. LeBron James's gold followed his first NBA title by just three weeks, completing what he called the best summer of his professional life to that point. Kevin Durant, 23 years old at the time, emerged from the tournament regarded as a potential future cornerstone of USA Basketball.

The 2012 Olympics also saw extraordinary individual athletic performances elsewhere in the programme: [Michael Phelps became the most decorated Olympian in history]({{ '/news/history-politics/michael-phelps-2012-olympics/' | relative_url }}) in the pool, while [Usain Bolt successfully defended his sprint titles]({{ '/news/history-politics/usain-bolt-100m-olympic-gold-2012/' | relative_url }}) on the track.

## Key Facts

- Tournament dates: July 29 – August 12, 2012, at the North Greenwich Arena (O2 Arena), London
- USA's victory margin in the gold-medal game: 7 points (107–100), the closest game of their tournament
- Carmelo Anthony's U.S. Olympic single-game scoring record: 37 points vs. Nigeria, August 2
- USA's tournament record: 8–0, average margin of victory approximately 32 points
- Bronze: Russia, defeating Argentina 81–77
