# Michael Phelps's 2012 Olympic Swimming Performance

**Category**: History & Politics  
**Key figures**: Michael Phelps (USA), Ryan Lochte (USA), Chad le Clos (South Africa), Yannick Agnel (France), Nathan Adrian (USA), Tyler Clary (USA)

## Summary

Michael Phelps competed at the 2012 London Summer Olympics, held at the Aquatics Centre in Olympic Park, after a brief retirement and public controversies following the 2008 Beijing Games. Entering London aged 27, Phelps swam six events — three individual and three relays — and won six medals in total: four gold and two silver. His gold medals came in the 4×200-meter freestyle relay (July 31), the 200-meter individual medley (August 2), the 100-meter butterfly (August 3), and the 4×100-meter medley relay (August 4). He won silver in the 4×100-meter freestyle relay (July 29, edged by France) and the 200-meter butterfly (July 31, defeated by Chad le Clos of South Africa by 0.05 seconds). He also swam the 400-meter individual medley on July 28, finishing fourth — his first failure to reach the podium in an Olympic final since the 2000 Sydney Games.

Phelps surpassed Soviet gymnast Larisa Latynina's previously unbroken record of 18 Olympic medals during the Games: his 200-meter butterfly silver on July 31 tied the mark at 18, and roughly an hour later his gold in the 4×200-meter freestyle relay — his 19th career medal — broke it, making him the most decorated Olympian in history. By the close of London 2012 his cumulative Olympic total reached 22 (18 gold, 2 silver, 2 bronze); Latynina had set the previous record of 18 across three Games (1956–1964).

## Background and Comeback

After Beijing 2008, Phelps announced an informal retirement from elite competition and admitted to marijuana use following the emergence of a photograph in early 2009. He returned to competitive swimming in 2010 but, by his own later account, struggled with motivation and depression through 2011. He publicly announced his intention to compete in London in 2011, hiring performance coach Bob Bowman — his long-time trainer since age 11 — to prepare him for a final Olympic cycle.

Phelps was beaten by American teammate Ryan Lochte in the 400-meter individual medley at the 2012 U.S. Olympic Trials. At London he opened his program with that event on July 28 and finished a surprising fourth (4:09.28), behind Lochte's gold — his first failure to medal in an Olympic final since the 2000 Sydney Games. The 200-meter individual medley and the butterfly events subsequently became the centerpieces of his medal haul.

## Rivalry with Ryan Lochte

Lochte, who trained at the University of Florida under coach Gregg Troy, and Phelps, coached throughout his career by Bob Bowman, were the two dominant figures of American men's swimming and fierce rivals. At London, Lochte won five medals — gold in the 400-meter individual medley and the 4×200-meter freestyle relay, silver in the 200-meter individual medley and the 4×100-meter freestyle relay, and bronze in the 200-meter backstroke. Their parallel campaigns attracted enormous media attention, and NBC framed the rivalry as one of the narrative threads of the Games.

Phelps won more medals than Lochte, six to five. The pair met head-to-head in the two medley events: Lochte beat Phelps to win the 400-meter IM on the opening day, with Phelps finishing fourth, while Phelps beat Lochte to take the 200-meter IM. The dynamic illustrated the depth of American men's swimming even as Phelps remained the marquee figure.

## Record-Breaking Context

Phelps's 19th Olympic medal — won as part of the 4×200-meter freestyle relay team on July 31 — broke Latynina's 48-year-old record. He had tied the mark at 18 with his 200-meter butterfly silver about an hour earlier the same evening. Larisa Latynina attended the London Games and publicly congratulated Phelps on surpassing her record. He went on to add three further golds, closing the Games with 22 career medals in all.

His 18 Olympic gold medals also remain the single-athlete record for gold medals in any sport. The next-nearest athletes — Latynina, Finnish distance runner Paavo Nurmi, American swimmer Mark Spitz, and American sprinter Carl Lewis — each won nine.

## Significance

Phelps's London campaign reinforced the value of sustained athletic longevity and specialisation. Swimming's race format — multiple heats and finals compressed into a single Games — demands extraordinary physiological recovery; Phelps, 27, managed a program many elite swimmers half his age would avoid. His 2012 performance also underscored the depth of American swimming: [teammate Nathan Adrian won the 100-meter freestyle gold](london-2012-olympics.md), and Ryan Lochte won five medals of his own.

The Games took place within the broader spectacle of [London 2012](london-2012-olympics.md), which showcased remarkable athletic achievement across multiple sports. On the track, [Usain Bolt defended his 100-meter title](usain-bolt-100m-olympic-gold-2012.md), and in basketball, [the USA men's team swept to gold undefeated](2012-olympics-mens-basketball.md).

After London, Phelps retired again, re-emerged for the 2016 Rio Games at age 31, and added six more medals (five gold, one silver) before retiring permanently with 28 Olympic medals in total.

## Key Facts

- Career Olympic medal total after London 2012: 22 (18 gold, 2 silver, 2 bronze)
- Record-breaking medal: his 19th career medal — gold in the 4×200-meter freestyle relay, July 31, 2012 — surpassed Latynina's 18
- Previous record holder for most Olympic medals: Soviet gymnast Larisa Latynina, 18 medals (1956–1964)
- Closest defeat: 200-meter butterfly, lost to Chad le Clos by 0.05 seconds (Phelps 1:53.01, le Clos 1:52.96)
- Phelps's trainer since age 11: Bob Bowman, North Baltimore Aquatic Club
- London 2012 Aquatics Centre designer: Zaha Hadid
